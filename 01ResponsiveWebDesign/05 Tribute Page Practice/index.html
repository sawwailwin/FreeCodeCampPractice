Bill Gates

The man who started Microsoft

Here's a time line of Bill Gates's life:

  • 1955: Born William Henry Gates III in Seattle, Washington
  • 1967: Attends Lakeside School, where he meets Paul Allen.
  • 1973: Enrolls at Harvard University.
  • 1975: Drops out of Harvard and co-founds Microsoft with Paul Allen
  • 1980: Microsoft signs a crucial deal with IBM to provide the Disk Operating System (DOS).
  • 1987: Becomes the youngest billionaire in the U.S. at age 31 after Microsoft's 1986 initial public offering (IPO). He also meets Melinda French at Microsoft.
  • 1994: Marries Melinda French.
  • 1995-2007: Forbes magazine ranks him as the world's wealthiest person for 13 consecutive years.
  • 1999: Becomes the first centibillionaire as his net worth surpasses $100 billion.
  • 2000: Establishes the Bill and Melinda Gates Foundation
  • 2007: Receives an honorary degree from Harvard University.
  • 2008: Retires from his day-to-day role at Microsoft to dedicate his time to the Bill and Melinda Gates Foundation.
